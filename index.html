The Signal

Chapter 1 · 12 min read

The radio telescope had been silent for seventy-three days. Dr. Helena Voss had grown accustomed to the static — it was the only companion in the high-altitude observatory. But tonight, the static changed.

A low, rhythmic pulse emerged from the noise. It wasn’t random. It wasn’t natural. It repeated every 4.7 seconds, like a heartbeat from the edge of the galaxy.

“That’s impossible,” she whispered, her breath fogging the cold glass of the spectrograph. The signal was coming from a region of space that had been dead for billions of years — a supernova remnant, the ghost of a star.

She adjusted the gain. The pulse grew louder, and with it came a sensation she couldn’t name — a pressure behind her eyes, a taste of iron in her mouth. The whisper.

And then the whisper spoke.
